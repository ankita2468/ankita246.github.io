DASHAIN

Dashain is a major Hindu religious festival celebrated in Nepal, Sikkim, and West Bengal, India. It is also celebrated by Hindus of Nepal and elsewhere in the world, including among the Lhotshampa of Bhutan and the Burmese Gurkhas of Myanmar. The festival lasts for fifteen days and generally falls in September or October, depending on the lunar calendar. It commemorates the goddess Durgaâ€™s conquest of the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the victory of righteousness over malevolence. During Dashain, Nepalese Hindus worship various forms of Durga, visit Shakti Pithas and pandals, organize plays, visit relatives, have feasts, community gatherings, recite scriptures, immerse the idol Durga or burn Ravana. The festival is also referred to as Nauratha, derived from the Sanskrit word for the same festival Navaratri which translates to Nine Nights.
